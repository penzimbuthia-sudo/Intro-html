Chess

Chess is a strategic board game played between two players on a 64-square board. Each player controls sixteen pieces, including a king, queen, rooks, bishops, knights, and pawns. The objective of the game is to checkmate the opponent’s king so it cannot escape capture. Chess requires careful thinking, planning, and patience because every move can affect the outcome of the game. Players must predict their opponent’s moves and create strong strategies. The game improves concentration, problem-solving, and decision-making skills. Played around the world, chess is both a competitive sport and a recreational activity that challenges the mind and encourages logical thinking. ♟️
